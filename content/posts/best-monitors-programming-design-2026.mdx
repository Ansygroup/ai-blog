### What to Look For
When shopping for a monitor for programming and design, there are several key factors to consider. First and foremost, resolution is crucial, as it directly affects the amount of screen real estate available. A higher resolution, such as 4K or QHD, provides more space to work with multiple windows and projects simultaneously. Additionally, the type of panel used, such as IPS or VA, can impact color accuracy and viewing angles.

Another important consideration is connectivity, as you'll want to ensure that your monitor has the necessary ports to connect to your devices. This may include USB-C, HDMI, and DisplayPort, depending on your specific setup. Ergonomics are also essential, as a monitor with adjustable height, tilt, and swivel can help reduce eye strain and improve comfort during long work sessions.

Finally, consider the refresh rate and response time, particularly if you plan to use your monitor for gaming or other graphics-intensive applications. A higher refresh rate, such as 144Hz or 240Hz, can provide a smoother visual experience, while a lower response time can reduce ghosting and blurring.
