### What to Look For
When selecting a monitor for programming and design, there are several key factors to consider. Firstly, the display's resolution and panel type play a crucial role in determining image quality. A 4K UHD or QHD display with an IPS or Nano IPS panel is ideal for tasks that require color accuracy and a high level of detail. Additionally, a high refresh rate and fast response time are essential for smooth performance, especially when working with graphics-intensive applications.

The connectivity options and ergonomics of a monitor are also important considerations. A monitor with a USB-C hub, HDMI 2.1, and DisplayPort 1.4 can provide a convenient and versatile connection setup. Furthermore, features like adjustable height, tilt, and swivel can help promote comfortable working posture and reduce eye strain. For professionals who require precise color calibration, a monitor with factory-calibrated colors and a wide color gamut is a must-have.
