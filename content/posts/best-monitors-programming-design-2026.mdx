### What to Look For
When shopping for a monitor for programming and design, there are several key factors to consider, including display size, resolution, and panel type. A larger display with a high resolution, such as 4K or QHD, provides more screen real estate and sharper text, making it easier to work on multiple projects simultaneously. Additionally, look for monitors with IPS or OLED panels, which offer better color accuracy and wider viewing angles.

For programmers, a monitor with a high refresh rate and fast response time is crucial for reducing eye strain and improving overall productivity. A USB-C port with Power Delivery (PD) is also a must-have, as it allows for easy connectivity and charging of devices. Designers, on the other hand, may prioritize color accuracy and gamut, seeking monitors that cover a wide range of colors, such as DCI-P3 or Adobe RGB.

In terms of ergonomics, consider a monitor with adjustable height, tilt, and swivel, as well as a VESA mount for easy installation on a wall or arm. Some monitors also come with built-in features like blue light filtering, flicker-free technology, and ambient light sensors, which can help reduce eye strain and improve overall comfort.
