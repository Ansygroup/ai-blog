When buying a laptop for AI and development, consider the processor, memory, and storage capacity. Additionally, ample memory (at least 16GB) and storage (at least 512GB) ensure that you can run multiple applications simultaneously without experiencing performance issues.

The display quality is also crucial, as it affects your overall user experience. Look for laptops with high-resolution displays, such as the 14.2-inch Liquid Retina XDR Display on the Apple MacBook Pro 14" M5 Pro (24GB/512GB). Furthermore, consider the connectivity options, including Thunderbolt ports, Wi-Fi 7, and USB-A ports, to ensure that you can connect your peripherals and accessories seamlessly.

Another important factor is the operating system, as some AI and development tools are exclusive to specific platforms. For example, macOS is ideal for developers working with Apple devices, while Windows 11 is suitable for those using Microsoft-based tools. Consider your specific needs and choose a laptop that aligns with your workflow.
