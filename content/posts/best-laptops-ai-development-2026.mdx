## What to Look For
When buying a laptop for AI and development, there are several key factors to consider. First, the processor is crucial, as it will handle the bulk of your workload. Look for laptops with the latest Intel Core Ultra or Apple M5 series chips, which offer significant performance boosts. Additionally, consider the amount of memory and storage you need, as AI and development [work](/posts/how-to-make-money-with-ai) often require large datasets and complex [models](/posts/microsoft-frontier-tuning-2026-custom-ai-models-rl).

The display is also an important consideration, as a high-quality screen can make a big difference in your overall user experience. Look for laptops with high-resolution displays, such as 4K or Retina, and consider the size of the screen. A larger screen can be beneficial for multitasking, but may also add to the overall weight and size of the laptop. Other features to consider include Wi-Fi 7 connectivity, Thunderbolt 5 ports, and [long](/posts/how-to-use-ai-to-summarize-long-documents-2026) battery life.

Another key factor is the operating [system](/posts/building-rag-system-langchain), as some AI and development tools are only compatible with certain OS. Windows 11, macOS, and Linux are popular options, each with their own strengths and weaknesses. Consider the specific tools and software you will be using and choose a laptop with an OS that supports them. Finally, consider your budget, as laptops for AI and development can range from under $1000 to over $5000.
