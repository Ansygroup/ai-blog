### What to Look For
When buying a laptop for AI and development, there are several key factors to consider. First and foremost, the processor is crucial, with Intel Core Ultra and AMD Ryzen 7 being popular choices. Additionally, a sufficient amount of RAM (at least 32GB) and storage (preferably a fast NVMe SSD) are necessary to handle large datasets and complex computations. A dedicated graphics card like the NVIDIA RTX 5070 can also be beneficial for tasks like deep learning and computer vision.

Another important aspect is the display, with options ranging from FHD to UHD+ and even OLED panels. A high-refresh-rate display (such as 120Hz or 240Hz) can be useful for tasks that require smooth visuals. Furthermore, consider the laptop's connectivity options, including Wi-Fi 7, Thunderbolt 4, and USB-C ports. The operating system, either Windows 11 or a Linux distribution, should also be taken into account.

Lastly, the laptop's design and build quality are essential for longevity and portability. Look for laptops with a sturdy construction, comfortable keyboard, and long battery life (at least 12 hours). Some laptops, like the Dell XPS 13 9350, also feature AI-powered cooling systems and advanced security features.
