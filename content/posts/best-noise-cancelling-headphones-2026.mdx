### What to Look For
When buying noise-cancelling headphones, there are several factors to consider, including sound quality, comfort, noise cancellation technology, and battery life. A good pair of noise-cancelling headphones should be able to effectively block out background noise, allowing you to focus on your work. Additionally, they should be comfortable to wear for extended periods, with features such as cushioned earpads and adjustable headbands.

The type of noise cancellation technology used is also an important consideration. Active noise cancellation, which uses microphones to capture and cancel out ambient noise, is generally more effective than passive noise cancellation, which relies on physical barriers to block out sound. Some headphones also offer advanced features such as adaptive noise cancellation, which adjusts to different environments and activities.

Another key factor to consider is battery life, as you'll want headphones that can last throughout the day without needing to be recharged. Some headphones offer up to 60 hours of battery life, while others may need to be recharged more frequently. It's also worth considering additional features such as wireless connectivity, voice assistant integration, and water resistance.
