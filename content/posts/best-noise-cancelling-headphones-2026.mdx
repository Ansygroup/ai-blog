### What to Look For
When shopping for noise-cancelling headphones, there are several key factors to consider, including the type of noise cancellation technology used, battery life, and sound quality. Active noise cancellation, which uses microphones to capture and cancel out ambient noise, is generally considered to be more effective than passive noise cancellation, which relies on physical barriers to block out sound. Additionally, look for headphones with long battery life, such as the Sennheiser Momentum 4 Wireless Noise Cancelling Headphones, which offer up to 60 hours of playback on a single charge.

Another important consideration is the type of audio codec used, as well as the overall sound quality. If you're an audiophile, you may want to look for headphones that support high-resolution audio codecs like LDAC or aptX HD, such as the Sony WF-1000XM5 Wireless Earbuds. Comfort is also a key factor, as you'll likely be wearing your headphones for extended periods of time. Look for headphones with soft, breathable materials and adjustable headbands, like the Bose QuietComfort Ultra Headphones.

Finally, consider the additional features that are important to you, such as wireless connectivity, voice assistant support, and water resistance.
